The question I hear most right now is some version of this: "Should I make a move with my money this year?"

Not "how do I invest" or "what's the market doing." Just — is this the year? Should I?

I understand the energy behind it. The Fire Horse year has a pull to it. You feel it. The sense that something is opening, that opportunity is moving through the air faster than it usually does. That's not your imagination. That's the year.

But here's what I've learned in over 35 years of readings: the Fire Horse rewards movement. It does not reward random movement. There's a difference between someone who steps forward with clarity and someone who lunges because the energy pushed them. One of them wins. The other one learns an expensive lesson.

Before you make any significant financial move this year — and I mean anything: a new business, a property, a big investment, a career bet that affects your income — you need to ask yourself four questions. These are the four angles of The Mahjong Mirror, and when applied to money, they cut through the noise fast.

## The First Angle: What Is the Central Theme Here?

This is the most important question, and it's the one most people skip entirely.

The central theme is not "I want to make money." That's a surface answer. Dig down. Why does this particular financial move call to you? What is it really about?

A client came to me a few years back asking about a business opportunity. Good numbers on paper. Reasonable risk. On the surface, an easy decision. But when we laid out the cards and looked at the central theme, what emerged was not a business story. It was a freedom story. She was trapped in a job she despised and saw this business as an exit door. The decision wasn't about the business at all — it was about reclaiming her life.

That reframe changed everything. Instead of analyzing the business on its financial merits alone, we looked at whether it would actually deliver the freedom she needed. Different question. Different answer.

Apply this to your own financial thinking right now. If you're considering a risk this year, ask yourself: what does this decision represent to me at the deepest level? Security? Independence? Proving something to yourself? A long-delayed beginning? The central theme tells you what you're actually deciding. Everything else is detail.

## The Second Angle: Who Do You Need to Be?

This is where honest self-knowledge either sets you up to win or saves you from a disaster you wouldn't have seen coming.

The Fire Horse year has a specific energy profile. It rewards the bold, the prepared, and the decisive. It is unforgiving of the timid and the unprepared in equal measure. Before you put money into motion, you need to know which category you're in — not which category you wish you were in, but which one you actually are right now.

Ask yourself three things.

First: Are you prepared, or are you riding the energy of the year? Preparation means you've done the work — the research, the planning, the stress-testing. The Snake year of 2025 was the year for that preparation. If you did it, 2026 is yours to move. If you spent 2025 coasting, moving fast in 2026 is not bold. It's reckless.

Second: Can you handle a fast-moving situation? The Fire Horse doesn't slow down for indecisive people. Opportunities in this year appear and disappear quickly. If you need weeks to process and deliberate before acting, that's useful information. It doesn't mean you can't win this year — it means you have to structure your moves so the timing pressure isn't crushing you.

Third: What is your relationship with money at a fundamental level? I've seen people with solid plans undermine themselves because, at their core, they didn't believe they deserved financial success. That's not astrology — that's human nature. The cards surface it. And if it's there, you have to look at it honestly before you act.

## The Third Angle: What Is the Opposition?

Every significant financial move has something working against it. The mistake is refusing to look at it clearly.

In a Mahjong Mirror reading, the Third Angle asks you to name the obstacles honestly — both the external ones and the ones you're carrying around inside. External opposition might be timing, competition, capital constraints, or a market that isn't ready. Internal opposition is trickier. It includes fear, overconfidence, scattered focus, or the tendency to overextend.

The Fire Horse year has one signature trap: it makes you feel like you have unlimited energy. The year itself has that quality — fast, electric, unstoppable. People absorb that energy and start making commitments that their actual human capacity cannot sustain. Burnout from overextension is the number one financial risk I see this year, across every sign, every background, every income level.

Name your opposition before you act. What could go wrong? What do you know about yourself that has gotten in your way before? What external factor are you hoping won't be a problem even though you suspect it might be?

The point is not to talk yourself out of moving. The point is to move with your eyes open.

## The Fourth Angle: What Does the Future Look Like?

This is where you test the decision against probability, not hope.

I'm not going to tell you I can guarantee outcomes. Nobody can. But I can tell you that after thousands of readings, there is a consistent difference between people who are aligned with a decision and people who are just excited about it. Aligned people have answered the first three questions honestly. Excited people have skipped them.

With money decisions in the Fire Horse year specifically, the future angle asks: does this move serve a 3-year picture, not just a 3-month one? The Fire Horse energy is so immediate and compelling that it's easy to optimize for right now and ignore what comes after. The best financial decisions I've seen in volatile years are the ones made with at least a medium-range view. Where does this lead you? Not just next quarter — next phase of life.

## What the Mirror Actually Does

I want to be clear about something. The Mahjong Mirror framework is not fortune telling. It's not a prediction machine. It's a decision-making framework that forces you to look at your situation from four angles you might otherwise miss.

Most people approach a financial decision with one angle: is this a good opportunity? The Mirror says — that's the least interesting question. The more important questions are: what is this really about, who do I need to be to execute it, what's standing in my way, and where does this lead me?

When all four angles are examined honestly, the right answer tends to become clear. Not because the cards told you — but because you finally looked at the whole picture.

The Fire Horse year is a real wealth opportunity. I said it on Monday and I'll say it again. Fire applied to earth produces metal, and metal is synonymous with money and wealth in Chinese philosophy. This is not a year to sit on the sidelines out of habit.

But movement without clarity is a gamble. Movement with clarity is a strategy.

The distinction between those two things is what the Mirror was built to show you.
