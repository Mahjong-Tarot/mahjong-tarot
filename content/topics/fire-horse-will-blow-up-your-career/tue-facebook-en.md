# Tuesday Facebook EN — The Fire Horse Will Blow Up Your Career

The people most at risk in a Fire Horse year aren't the ones who hate their jobs. It's the ones who say "it's fine."

I want to explain why, because it's not intuitive.

The person who hates their job is already awake to the problem. They're asking questions. They're looking for exits. They're already in motion, even if slowly. When the Fire Horse disrupts their career — and it will — the disruption tends to go somewhere. Out the door. Into something new. Toward what they'd been avoiding saying out loud.

The "it's fine" person is in a different situation. They've made peace with something that doesn't actually fit. The Life Palace says one thing. The job says another. The Fortune & Virtue Palace is pointing in a direction they stopped paying attention to years ago. And they've gotten so good at managing the gap that they've stopped noticing it.

Fine is a kind of sleep.

The Fire Horse wakes you up. One way or another. It finds the dimension you've been ignoring and it turns up the volume until you can't ignore it anymore.

The question is whether you're going to wake up on your own terms or wait to be woken up by a restructuring announcement, a conflict that escalated too fast, a role that suddenly doesn't fit in ways it somehow didn't matter last year.

This is why I keep writing about this year's career energy. Not to alarm anyone — to give people enough of a heads-up that they can get ahead of it instead of reacting to it.

What's the thing in your work life you've made peace with that maybe you shouldn't have?
