# Monday Facebook EN — Opposites Attract Is a Lie

"Opposites attract" is one of the biggest misconceptions about love. And I say that as a Pig who has fallen for the Snake's charm more times than I'd like to admit.

The Snake is everything the Pig tends to lack. Deep, intelligent, a little mysterious. You're never quite sure what's behind the eyes. For some reason, that makes you want to look harder. I've seen it in hundreds of readings. I've felt it myself.

And I can tell you from 35 years of practice: attraction isn't compatibility.

The Pig and the Snake are opposing signs. The six opposing pairs in the Chinese zodiac — Rat/Horse, Ox/Sheep, Tiger/Monkey, Rabbit/Rooster, Dragon/Dog, Snake/Pig — attract each other because the contrast feels like balance. What one has, the other lacks. That pull is real.

But having what your partner lacks isn't the same as being compatible with them. It's the same as being a temporary relief from yourself. And relief has a shelf life.

In a calm year, opposing-sign couples can manage the friction through chemistry and goodwill. The long-term success probability is low, but it's possible.

In a Fire Horse year — where everything runs hotter and faster — the friction that was quiet becomes loud. The differences that were charming become the issue. Cracks become canyons.
