You don't need a card reading to use the Mahjong Mirror framework. That's the whole point of the book.

Four angles of reflection you can apply to any financial decision, starting right now — no tiles required.

The first angle asks what the move is really about at its core. The second asks who you need to be to execute it. The third asks what's standing in your way — honestly, including the things you're hoping won't matter. The fourth asks where this leads you in the medium term, not just next month.

Most people skip all four and jump straight to: is the opportunity good? The Mirror says that's the least interesting question.

What makes a move right or wrong isn't just the opportunity. It's whether you, at this moment, are aligned with it — prepared for it, clear about it, honest about the obstacles.

That's the difference between a bold move and a reckless one. And in a Fire Horse year, that difference is everything.
