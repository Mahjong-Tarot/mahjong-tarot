Feel Good Friday: If the Fire Horse year is amplifying frustration in your career, that's not a curse. It's a signal. You're ready for something bigger. Name it.

I had a client this year who was certain she needed to change jobs. Seven years at the same company. Everything was fine. But this year, everything felt louder — the commute, the meetings, the gap between what she was doing and what she wanted to do.

She thought the year was making things worse.

It wasn't. The Fire Horse doesn't create problems. It amplifies what's already there. The frustration she was feeling wasn't new. It had been there for years, manageable, quiet. The year turned up the volume.

And the volume was pointing somewhere. Not away from her company — toward something she'd been sitting on for years: the chance to lead something herself.

She didn't quit. She built a proposal, pitched it, and got the green light. Six months later, the frustration was gone.

This weekend: write down "What I actually want is..." and finish the sentence.

Not what seems realistic. What you actually want.
