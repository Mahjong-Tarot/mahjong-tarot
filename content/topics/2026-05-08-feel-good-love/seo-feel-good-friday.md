## FAQ Schema
Q: What does balance mean in a relationship according to Chinese astrology?
A: In Chinese astrology, relationship balance refers to complementary yin-yang energy and a distribution of the five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) between two partners. Balanced couples support each other's energetic needs without one person consistently overextending. In the Fire Horse year, imbalance tends to surface quickly.

Q: How can I tell if my relationship is healthy during the Fire Horse year?
A: Signs of a healthy relationship during the Fire Horse year include: genuine knowledge of your partner's real needs, mutual authenticity (not just showing the good parts), the ability to argue without withdrawing permanently, regular meaningful conversation, and the ongoing daily choice to prioritize the relationship.

Q: What is a Feel Good Friday challenge from Bill Hajdu?
A: Each Friday, Bill Hajdu (The Firepig) ends the week with an actionable prompt. Something the reader can do over the weekend to apply the week's themes to their real life. The challenge is always specific, positive, and tied to the week's Fire Horse and Mahjong Mirror content.

Q: Is the Fire Horse year bad for relationships?
A: Not necessarily. The Fire Horse year is an amplifier. Relationships with genuine health and balance often move to the next level. Strained relationships face higher risk. The key is honest self-assessment. The Mahjong Mirror's Second Angle provides a framework for that.

Q: What is "tend the garden" in Bill Hajdu's relationship advice?
A: "Tend the garden" is Bill Hajdu's metaphor for active, ongoing care of a relationship. Just as a garden requires daily attention to thrive (not grand gestures, but consistent watering, pruning, and presence), a relationship requires regular small acts of choosing your partner over distraction, comfort, and avoidance.
