# Two Snakes Can't Make It Work — Right?

**Category:** Year of the Fire Horse  
**Author:** Bill Hajdu  
**Date:** 2026-04-14  
**Read Time:** 6 min  
**Excerpt:** Every Chinese astrology source says two Snakes is a bad match. Taylor and Travis are both Snakes. So why does their chart tell me they're going to make it?

---

Ask any Chinese astrology expert whether two Snakes should marry, and you'll get the same answer: no. The Snake is a Yin sign, but it's still a power sign. It wants to be dominant. Put two of them in a room and you've got two people quietly fighting to be No. 1. Not hostile. Not dramatic. Just two forces that both need the crown.

Now add that Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce are reportedly planning their wedding during a Fire Horse year — the single most volatile year in the 60-year Chinese astrological cycle — and most astrologers would tell you to cancel the caterer.

I'm the Firepig. I've been doing this for over 35 years. And I'm here to tell you the conventional wisdom is about 20% of the picture.

## The Myth: Two Snakes Is a Bad Match

Let's be honest about where this comes from. The Snake wants control. Not in a loud, aggressive way — the Snake operates through influence, strategy, quiet dominance. When two Snakes pair up, neither one wants to yield. That tension is real. Even AI-generated compatibility tools flag Snake-Snake as problematic by default.

And when you look at Taylor and Travis on the surface, you see exactly why. Taylor Swift is arguably the most successful recording artist alive. Travis Kelce is considered one of the greatest tight ends in NFL history. In this relationship, who emerges as No. 1? That tension is baked into their charts.

If you stopped the analysis right there — which most people do — you'd walk away shaking your head. Two power players, one relationship, no room for both to lead.

But here's the thing. Most people stop at the animal sign. And the animal sign is only the beginning.

## What Most People Forget: The Element Changes Everything

This is where I break from the crowd. Taylor and Travis aren't just two Snakes. They're both **Earth Snakes** — born in the same Earth year, 1989. That shared Earth element changes the entire equation.

When I ran the [Four Pillars chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Four_Pillars_of_Destiny), something interesting happened. Even the AI compatibility rating — which usually flags two Snakes as trouble — climbed significantly once the Earth element entered the picture. Now, I don't fully agree with the AI's exact score. But I agree with the direction. The shared Earth grounds what would otherwise be a volatile pairing.

This is the lesson I keep teaching: **don't just look at the animal sign. Look at the element.** The sign tells you the personality. The element tells you how it actually plays out.

Two generic Snakes? Trouble. Two Earth Snakes? A very different story.

## Three Risks You Can't Ignore

Before I make the case for this couple, I need to be straight with you about the risks. They're real.

**Risk one: the Fire Horse year itself.** Of all 60 years in the Chinese cycle, the [Fire Horse year](/blog/posts/who-has-the-most-luck-in-the-fire-horse-year) has the highest probability of disasters. In Japanese culture, families traditionally avoid getting married — and even having children — during Fire Horse years. Do you really want to plan one of the biggest events of your life in the year most likely to blow it up?

**Risk two: quadruple fire.** They're both Earth Fire Snakes, born the same year. Add that to a Fire Horse year and you have quadruple fire energy. Temperaments flare more easily. Issues crop up that wouldn't exist in a calmer year, and they get blown out of proportion. Arguments become things you can't walk back.

**Risk three: the career imbalance.** After the wedding, Travis heads into what is likely his final NFL season — already past his peak. Taylor is at the absolute height of her career. One person is ascending; the other is transitioning into a second act. When one partner's fire is a positive force and the other's is burning out, that imbalance creates real pressure. In a [Fire Horse year](/blog/posts/love-in-the-fire-horse-year), there's no in between — it's either a recipe for disaster or massive success.

## The Pivot: Fire Horse Isn't Good or Bad

This is where the [Mahjong Mirror](/the-mahjong-mirror) framework comes in. We don't speak in terms of good and bad. We speak in terms of risk. The Fire Horse is fast, passionate, and chaotic. High risk, high reward. The question is never "is this a good year to get married?" The question is: **does the chart support the risk?**

And for Taylor and Travis, the answer surprised me.

## Why Their Chart Actually Works

**Their elements mirror each other.** Laid out across year, month, and day signs, Taylor's elements are Earth, Fire, Water. Travis's are Earth, Water, Fire. They're not arranged identically, but they mirror each other almost perfectly. In Chinese philosophy, that kind of balance is the foundation of sustained success.

**They carry three of four elements.** Between them, they hold Earth, Fire, and Water. Most couples are lopsided elementally. These two aren't. We don't have their hour of birth, so the full [Bazi chart](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Four_Pillars_of_Destiny) can't be completed — but what's visible is remarkably balanced.

**Four of their six signs are power signs.** Travis's three signs — Snake, Rooster, Snake — all fall in the same compatibility group. That gives him natural inner peace and confidence. It's not something he works for. It's born into him. Taylor's signs — Dragon, Snake, Rat-Dragon — include two power signs plus a favorable combination. The charts confirm what everyone already senses: this is a genuine power couple. Most so-called power couples don't have the charts to back it up. These two do.

**And here's what elevates it.** This isn't just a love match. It's a business match. Taylor is already a media mogul. Travis is building a media career — one of the most successful podcasts going, a move into broadcasting. They're both power signs. They're elementally balanced. When you combine power with balance, that's rare. And it's what might make this work despite every factor stacked against them.

## The Wedding Date They Got Right by Accident

One more thing worth noting. The wedding date has reportedly been changed more than once. We don't know why. My read: dumb luck, not deliberate astrology consulting. But here's what a Chinese astrologer would notice — moving the date from June to July shifted it out of the Horse month, which is the worst month for relationships in a Fire Horse year, and into the Goat month. The Goat is the soulmate sign of the Horse. That's either coincidence or something larger at work.

A word to the wise: even when you don't know you're doing it, sometimes the universe moves you where you need to be.

## My Prediction

I'll go out on a limb. This relationship works. And it probably lasts a long time.

That's a bold call for celebrity couples — who have a train wreck track record. But the charts support it. The elements are balanced. The power is shared. And the business alignment gives them something most celebrity relationships don't have: a reason to build together, not just coexist.

The signs said no. The elements said maybe. The full chart says yes.
