# Feel Good Friday: The Best Relationship You'll Ever Have Starts in the Mirror

A woman sat across the table from me a few years back. Smart. Put-together. The kind of person you'd assume had everything figured out. She wanted to know about a man she'd been seeing — whether the signs said they were compatible. Whether the tiles would tell her he was the one.

I laid the tiles out. And before we got to him, the Mirror showed me something she wasn't expecting.

It showed me her.

## The Reading She Didn't Ask For

Her Central Theme — the first tile, the one that reveals what the situation is actually about — didn't land on the relationship. It landed on identity. Not "who is this man to me?" but "who am I when I'm with him?"

She'd been so focused on finding the right person that she'd skipped a step. The step most people skip. She hadn't asked herself who she was — not the version she performed on dates, not the version she thought a partner wanted, but the woman who was actually sitting at the table.

I told her that. Plainly. The way I tell you things.

She didn't love hearing it.

But here's what happened next: she stopped asking about him. She started asking about herself. What she actually wanted. What patterns she kept repeating. What she was afraid of. And that conversation — not the one about the man — changed everything.

Six months later she came back. Different energy. Same woman, but she'd done the work. She wasn't looking for someone to complete her anymore. She was looking for someone who fit the life she was already building.

The tiles that day? They sang.

## The Second Angle: Know Thyself

This is what the Mahjong Mirror's Second Angle is about. Looking at Yourself. Not in a vague, journal-about-your-feelings way. In a structured, honest, sit-with-the-uncomfortable-truth way.

The Second Angle asks: Who are you? Who do you need to become? What do you truly want? And what's actually happening in your life right now — not what you wish was happening, not what you're pretending is happening, but what is?

If you've been reading along this week, you've seen these themes building.

On Monday, we looked at [Taylor Swift and Travis Kelce](/blog/posts/taylor-swift-travis-kelce-fire-horse-wedding) — two Earth Fire Snakes whose signs say "power struggle" but whose elements say "partnership." The lesson was clear: surface readings don't tell the whole story. You have to go deeper. Signs are the headline. Elements are the article. The full chart is where the truth lives.

On Wednesday, we explored [the Mahjong Mirror framework for wedding planning](/blog/posts/planning-a-wedding-through-the-mahjong-mirror) — how the First Angle reveals your Central Theme and the Fourth Angle asks what you really want from a marriage. The lesson there was about intention. A wedding without it is just a party. A marriage without it is just proximity.

But here's the thread that ties both days together, the one I've been building toward all week: none of it works if you haven't done the Second Angle first. Not compatibility analysis. Not wedding planning. Not even a really good tile reading. Because every question about love eventually circles back to the same starting point.

You.

## Why Self-Knowledge Is the Foundation

I'm the Firepig. In over 35 years of doing readings, I've sat across from thousands of people asking about love. And the pattern I see more than any other isn't bad compatibility. It isn't wrong timing. It isn't even the wrong partner.

It's people who don't know themselves well enough to recognize what's right when it shows up.

They chase chemistry because they haven't identified what they actually need. They repeat the same relationship with a different face because they haven't examined the pattern. They ask the tiles about someone else when the tiles are trying to show them something about themselves.

The woman I told you about? She wasn't unusual. She was the rule.

Taylor and Travis work — not because two Earth Fire Snakes are supposed to work, but because both of them seem to know exactly who they are. Taylor Swift didn't become the greatest recording artist of her generation by accident. Travis Kelce didn't become one of the greatest tight ends in NFL history by being unclear about his identity. They brought that same clarity to a relationship that, on paper, shouldn't function.

That's not luck. That's self-knowledge doing what it does. When you know who you are, you stop making decisions from fear. You stop performing a version of yourself that you think someone else wants. You show up as the person you actually are — and that person is either compatible with someone or they're not. But at least it's real.

## The Mirror Doesn't Judge

Here's what I want you to understand about the Second Angle: it's not about becoming perfect. It's about becoming honest.

The Mahjong Mirror doesn't look at you and say "you're broken" or "you're not ready." It reflects what's actually there. Your strengths. Your blind spots. The patterns you keep running. The fears you haven't named.

And when you see those things clearly — not to fix them on a deadline, but to acknowledge them — something shifts. Your decisions get sharper. Your relationships get realer. You stop asking "is this person right for me?" and start asking "am I showing up as someone who can be right for anyone?"

That second question is harder. It's also the one that matters.

## What the Week Taught Us

Monday showed us that compatibility is more complex than a headline. Two Snakes can build something extraordinary when the elements align. But you have to look past the surface to see it.

Wednesday showed us that intention transforms everything. A wedding planned from the inside out — starting with your Central Theme and what you really want — becomes a foundation, not just an event.

And today? Today is the piece that makes both of those work. Because you can't assess compatibility honestly if you don't know who you are. And you can't set real intentions if you haven't looked in the Mirror first.

The Second Angle is where it all begins.

> Before you can be the right partner, you have to know the person you're asking someone else to love.

## Your Weekend Challenge

I'm not going to ask you to overhaul your life this weekend. I'm going to ask you to do one thing.

Find a quiet moment. Maybe Sunday morning with your coffee. Maybe tonight before bed. And ask yourself one honest question:

**Who am I in my relationships — not who I want to be, not who I pretend to be, but who I actually am right now?**

Write the answer down. Don't edit it. Don't make it pretty. Just let it be true.

That's the Second Angle at work. That's the Mirror doing what it does. And whatever you see there — whether it makes you proud or makes you uncomfortable — that's the foundation. That's where real love gets built.

Not with finding someone. With knowing yourself well enough to show up as the person worth finding.

Tend the garden.

— Bill
