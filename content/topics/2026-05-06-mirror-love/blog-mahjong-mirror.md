You've been told that the key to a lasting relationship is finding the right person. The one who makes you feel it. The undeniable spark. The chemistry.

That belief isn't wrong. It's just about 30% of the picture.

I'm the Firepig, and in over 35 years of readings, I've sat across from more people in "chemistry-fueled" relationships that were quietly falling apart than I can count. The feeling was real. The connection felt electric. And the tiles on the table were telling a different story entirely.

Chemistry tells you how you feel. The Mahjong Mirror tells you what's actually there. In most years, you can live with the gap between those two things. In a Fire Horse year, that gap will be handed to you whether you asked for it or not.

## What the Myth Gets Right

Chemistry matters. I'm not dismissing it. The initial draw, the excitement, the sense that this person is unlike anyone else. That's real. And for certain combinations (Tiger-Dragon, Horse-Sheep, Dog-Rabbit), the natural energy between signs creates a kind of built-in pull that I've seen produce genuinely powerful relationships.

But chemistry is a measurement of how things feel right now. It can't tell you who you are in this relationship. It can't tell you who you need to become. It can't tell you whether you're bringing your actual self to the table or performing the version of yourself that this person draws out of you.

That's what the Mirror does.

## The Second Angle: Know Thyself

The Mahjong Mirror works through four angles of reflection. The First Angle identifies your central theme. The Third Angle examines your opposition. The Fourth Angle looks at your future.

The Second Angle is the one most people skip. It asks: Who am I?

Not who you are at your best. Not the aspirational version. Not the one you present on a first date or in the first year of a relationship. Who you actually are, at the core, right now.

The Second Angle unfolds through three questions:

**Who Am I?** Your core essence. Not personality traits or current mood. The unchanging nature that persists through every season of your life.

**What Do I Really Want?** Not what you think you should want. Not what your partner wants you to want. What you are actually reaching for underneath the surface.

**What's Happening Now?** Your current circumstances, your timing, your real situation. Not the story you're telling yourself about it.

When those three questions get honest answers, they produce something the chemistry question never can: clarity about who you need to be in order to make this relationship work.

## The Card That Changes the Conversation

A few years back, a woman sat across from me. She was in what most people would describe as a strong relationship: stable, long-term, no dramatic problems. But she'd had a restless feeling she couldn't name. She came in thinking the reading would confirm that everything was fine.

The Ducks card appeared in her core identity position.

The Ducks represent lasting partnership. Ducks mate for life. They migrate together. When the Ducks show up as your core identity, it means relationships are not just something you value. They are how you understand yourself. Your fullest expression comes through connection with another person.

I told her: "You're not someone who settles for 'fine' in a relationship. It's not in your nature. You need genuine, ongoing depth. Not just stability. Real partnership."

She was quiet for a long time. Then: "He doesn't know me. After six years, he doesn't actually know me."

That was the honest answer she came in hoping to avoid. The chemistry had been real once. The stability was real. But she had never shown her partner the person the Ducks card was pointing to: the one who needed real depth, not just coexistence. She'd settled for being known enough, and the restlessness was the price.

Chemistry had told her the relationship was workable. The Mirror showed her what was actually missing.

## What the Peach Card Means Right Now

In this Fire Horse year, I've been surprised by how often the Peach card is appearing in readings. The Peach represents romantic temptation. Affairs. The pull of someone new.

When the Peach shows up in a reading, it doesn't always mean an affair is happening. Sometimes it means the temptation is present and the person is aware of it. Sometimes it means the hunger for passion (for being truly seen and wanted) has become strong enough that outside attention is starting to feel like an answer.

The Peach appearing in readings this year isn't a verdict. It's a question: is the passion you need being met inside your relationship? If not, the Fire Horse year will amplify that gap into something harder to ignore.

That question is a Second Angle question. Not "is my partner satisfying me?" but "who am I in this relationship, and what do I actually need, and am I asking for it?"

## Chemistry Is the Start. The Mirror Is the Practice.

Here's the thing about the Second Angle: it's not a one-time question. It's ongoing. Who you are changes as you move through life. What you want evolves. Your current circumstances shift.

The Mirror is not a diagnostic tool you use once. It's a practice. The strongest relationships I've seen (the ones that hold up through volatile years like this one) are built by two people who keep asking themselves the Second Angle questions. Not because they're uncertain about the relationship, but because they stay honest about who they are.

Your partner fell in love with who you were. They stay in love with who you keep becoming. The Mirror keeps that process honest.

## What to Do Before the Year Forces the Conversation

If you've felt a version of the restlessness I described, if there's a gap between how your relationship feels and what it looks like from the outside, the Fire Horse year will not let that stay quiet.

You have two choices. You can wait for the year to surface it for you. Or you can hold up the mirror yourself.

Start with the three Second Angle questions:
- Who am I, really, right now?
- What do I actually want underneath the story I've been telling?
- What's the honest state of my relationship in this moment?

Those are not comfortable questions. They are useful ones.
