Career crossroads? The Mahjong Mirror asks four questions most people skip. The third one — what's really opposing you — is where the breakthrough hides.

Most career analysis is purely external: is the market right, is the company good, is the compensation fair? The Mahjong Mirror turns the lens inward first. What is this decision really about at its core? Who do you need to be to make it work? What is standing in your way — internally and externally? And where does this realistically lead?

That third question is the one I see people skip. Every. Single. Time.

They name the external obstacles — the difficult boss, the market shift, the resource problem. But the internal opposition? The overextension pattern. The tendency to quit at exactly the wrong moment. The unrealistic expectations that guarantee feeling like a failure even when you're succeeding? That's where most people look away.

The Mahjong Mirror was built to look where you don't want to.
