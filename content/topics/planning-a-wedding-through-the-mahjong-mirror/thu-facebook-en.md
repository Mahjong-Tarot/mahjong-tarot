The Fourth Angle of the Mahjong Mirror asks one question: What do you really want?

Not what you think you should want. Not what your family expects. Not what looks right on paper. What you actually want.

Most couples skip this during wedding planning. They plan the reception, the honeymoon, the seating chart. They forget to plan the intention behind all of it.

Here's what I've seen in 35 years of readings: the couples who last aren't the ones with the best compatibility charts. They're the ones who sat down — before the wedding, not after — and got honest about what they were building together.

"I want to feel safe enough to fail."
"I want someone who tells me the truth."
"I want to build something neither of us could build alone."

Those aren't vows. They're foundations.
