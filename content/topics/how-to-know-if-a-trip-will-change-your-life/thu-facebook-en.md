# Thursday Facebook EN — How to Know If a Trip Will Change Your Life

The first angle of the Mahjong Mirror is the Central Theme. Applied to travel, it reveals whether a trip will change your life or just change your scenery.

Yesterday I wrote about the two fundamentally different kinds of movement: toward something, or away from something. But there's a third thing the first angle shows that I didn't get to — and it's the one most people miss.

Your Central Theme in a decision isn't just about your motivation. It's also about your timing.

Sometimes a trip is the right move at the wrong time. The pull is genuine — you're not running from anything, you're genuinely drawn toward something. But the Central Theme reveals that you're not ready yet. There's something unresolved that needs to happen first before the trip can deliver what you're hoping it will.

In 35 years of readings, this is one of the most common patterns I see with travel questions. The person isn't wrong to want to go. The destination isn't wrong. The timing is off. And the Central Theme — the gravitational force shaping their current situation — is pulling them to deal with something closer to home first.

That's not a no. It's a not yet.

The difference between a life-changing trip and an expensive detour is often just that — knowing which one you're in. The first angle of the mirror tells you.
