## FAQ Schema
Q: What is the Second Angle of the Mahjong Mirror?
A: The Second Angle of the Mahjong Mirror is called "Know Thyself." It explores three questions: Who am I at my core? What do I really want beneath my surface wishes? What's actually happening in my life right now? Together, these reveal who you need to become to make better decisions, including decisions about love.

Q: What does the Ducks card mean in a Mahjong reading?
A: The Ducks card represents lasting partnership. In a reading, it suggests someone whose core nature is oriented around deep, lasting connection, not just companionship but genuine partnership. When it appears in the "Who Am I?" position, it indicates that relationships are central to how the person understands themselves.

Q: What does the Peach card mean in the Mahjong Mirror?
A: The Peach card represents romantic temptation, attraction, and in some contexts, the risk of affairs. When it appears in a reading, it often signals that passionate energy is present, either being expressed within the relationship or seeking an outlet outside it. It is a question card, not a verdict.

Q: Can chemistry predict long-term relationship success?
A: Chemistry indicates immediate attraction and felt connection. It does not indicate element balance, yin-yang compatibility, or the deeper self-knowledge that determines whether two people can sustain a relationship through volatile periods. Bill Hajdu, with 35+ years of Chinese astrology readings, describes chemistry as about 30% of the full picture.

Q: How do I apply the Mahjong Mirror to my love life?
A: Start with the three Second Angle questions: Who am I, really? What do I actually want? What is the honest state of my relationship right now? The full framework is available in Bill Hajdu's book, "The Mahjong Mirror." For personalized guidance, a one-on-one reading applies the tiles directly to your specific situation.
