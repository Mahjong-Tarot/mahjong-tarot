# Thursday Facebook EN — The Decision Framework for Career Crossroads

You don't need a card reading to use the Mahjong Mirror framework. That's the whole point of the book.

I want to be direct about this, because I think some people assume that the framework only works when I'm in the room reading the tiles. It doesn't.

The four angles are a structure for honest self-examination. The tiles add depth — they show things that are hard to access on your own because there's nothing to argue with when the cards speak. But the framework itself — the four questions, applied in sequence, given real answers — is completely accessible without a reading.

For career decisions specifically, here's what that looks like in practice.

You sit with the Central Theme question: what is this decision actually about at the level underneath the surface? Not "should I take the promotion" but "what's the real thing this crossroads is asking me to resolve?" You write the answer down. You don't edit it. You read what you actually wrote.

Then you do the Looking at Yourself angle: who am I in this situation? Who do I need to become? What do I actually want? What's honestly happening right now? You answer all four, not just the comfortable ones.

Then the Opposition: what's actually blocking me? And you push past the first answer, which is usually external and usually partially true. You go deeper: what am I carrying internally that's reinforcing this situation?

Then the Future: if I make this move, where does it probably take me? If I don't, where does that path likely lead?

Four questions. One sitting. More clarity than most people generate in months of circular thinking.
