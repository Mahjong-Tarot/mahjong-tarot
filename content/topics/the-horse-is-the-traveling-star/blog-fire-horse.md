# The Horse Is the Traveling Star — and It's on Fire

Let me tell you something about the year you're living in that most people completely miss.

In Purple Star Astrology — the system I've practiced for over 35 years — there is a specific heavenly stem called Ma. It's the traveling star. It signals movement, relocation, journeys that matter, displacement that changes things. When Ma appears in a reading, something is moving. A person, a situation, a life.

The traveling star is the Horse.

You're living in the Year of the Fire Horse.

I want you to sit with that for a moment. Not metaphorically. Literally. The traveling star itself is the ruling animal of this year — doubled with fire energy, which is the most expansive, outward-moving force in the five-element system. This is not a year when Ma makes a guest appearance in a reading. This is a year when Ma is the entire sky.

The best travel year in a 60-year cycle is happening right now. If you're not moving, you're not just missing an opportunity. You're actively swimming against the strongest current of travel energy that anyone alive today has ever experienced.

## What the Traveling Star Actually Does

In Purple Star Astrology, the traveling star doesn't just mean vacation. It means displacement of any kind — physical, psychological, professional. It's the energy behind moves that change your trajectory. The person who relocates for an opportunity. The one who takes the trip that becomes a turning point. The couple whose relationship is cemented not by a ring but by a plane ticket they buy together.

Ma energy in a quiet year is a nudge. It favors the people who act on it and largely ignores the ones who don't. But in a Fire Horse year, when the animal of the traveling star is the year's ruling energy and fire is pouring fuel on top of it — Ma becomes a force of nature. It doesn't nudge. It pulls.

The people I'm watching move this year aren't the adventurers by nature. They're accountants and teachers and parents of three kids who couldn't explain why they suddenly bought tickets to Vietnam. They're retired couples who haven't gone anywhere in years who are calling me to say they're moving to Portugal. They're entrepreneurs who've been talking about expanding internationally for a decade who just signed a lease in Singapore.

That's Ma on fire. That's what this year actually feels like from the inside.

## Why the Horse Makes Travel Different

The Horse is Yang. Masculine. Outward. Freedom-seeking. It doesn't strategize, it moves. It doesn't analyze the route, it runs. And it doesn't run toward comfort — it runs toward what's open, what's new, what's uncharted.

In practical terms, what that means for this year is that the best travel isn't the kind that's perfectly planned. The Fire Horse rewards movement, not preparation. The person who books the trip impulsively and figures out the details later is working with the energy. The person who's been planning a big trip for two years and still hasn't pulled the trigger is working against it.

This isn't my personality talking. I'm a military intelligence officer by training — I plan for a living. But I've watched the Fire Horse long enough to know that this is the one year where you have to trust the impulse, get in motion, and let the path reveal itself. The Horse doesn't wait for you to be ready.

## The 60-Year Context

I want to put this in perspective, because I think people hear "the year of the Fire Horse" and assume it's marketing language. It isn't. The 60-year cycle of Chinese astrology is literal — every combination of the twelve animals and five elements appears exactly once in 60 years. The Fire Horse last appeared in 1966. Before that, 1906.

In both of those years, the historical record is full of extraordinary movement. Migrations. Relocations. Expeditions. Discoveries made by people who left where they were. 1966 alone produced more significant cross-cultural encounters, voluntary relocations, and life-changing journeys per capita than almost any year in modern memory.

We are in that year again. And if you are reading this in 2026, you will not see another one in your lifetime.

## The Question Worth Asking

I'm not telling you to abandon your life and buy a one-way ticket somewhere. The Fire Horse isn't asking for recklessness. It's asking for movement. The distinction matters.

A day trip counts. A long weekend in a city you've never visited counts. Saying yes to the conference in another country that you'd normally decline because it's inconvenient — that counts. The Horse doesn't care about distance. It cares about direction. Are you moving, or are you staying still?

The people who will look back on this year as a turning point are the ones who moved. Not necessarily far, not necessarily forever. But they moved.

Where are you going?
