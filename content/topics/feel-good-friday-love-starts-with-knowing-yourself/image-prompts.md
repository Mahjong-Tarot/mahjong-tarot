# Image Prompts — Feel Good Friday: Love Starts with Knowing Yourself

**Topic:** Self-knowledge as the foundation of real love. Looking honestly at yourself before you can show up fully in a relationship.

**Phrases:**
- "Know yourself first. Love follows."
- "Before you find the right person, be the right person."
- "The mirror doesn't judge. It reflects."
- "You can't show up for someone else until you show up for yourself."
- "Real love starts with an honest question."

## blog-feel-good-friday.md

**Concept:** A woman alone on a quiet Sunday morning — the private moment of honest self-reflection before the world asks anything of her.
**Image Style:** HUMAN — cinematic photograph, woman at kitchen table, seen from the side
**Aspect:** 16:9 (1200x630)

Prompt: A woman sits alone at a wooden kitchen table in soft, diffused morning light. She holds a ceramic mug with both hands. A journal lies open in front of her, pen resting beside it. She is seen from the side — never facing camera — her gaze turned slightly inward, mid-thought. The light is warm and natural, filtering through a sheer curtain. The room is simple, uncluttered. Warm ivory tones with touches of dusty rose in the fabric of her clothing. Film still aesthetic. No performance, no audience — just a woman being honest with herself. No watermarks or Western zodiac imagery anywhere in the image.

## fri-facebook-vn.md

**Concept:** Still water reflecting the sky — clarity only comes when you stop moving long enough to see yourself.
**Image Style:** SCENE — atmospheric nature, no people
**Aspect:** 16:9 (1200x630)

Prompt: A perfectly still lake or pond surface at early morning, reflecting a pale dawn sky above. The horizon line sits in the lower third of the frame so the reflection dominates. The water is glassy and near-perfect — sky and reflection nearly indistinguishable from each other. A single fallen leaf rests at the very edge of frame, the only mark of motion in an otherwise held-breath stillness. Palette: cool silver-blue water, soft ivory and gold at the horizon, muted sage at the far treeline. Wide angle, shot from just above the water surface. No people, no structures. The mood is introspective — what the world looks like when you finally stop rushing. No watermarks or Western zodiac imagery anywhere in the image.
