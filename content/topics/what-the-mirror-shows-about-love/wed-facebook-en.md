# Wednesday Facebook EN — What the Mirror Shows About Love

Chemistry tells you how you feel. The Mahjong Mirror tells you what's actually there.

The Second Angle — Know Thyself — is the one most people skip in love. It's the one that matters most.

Here's why. Chemistry is fast. You feel it or you don't. And in a Fire Horse year, you're feeling it strongly — the fire amplifies attraction, intensity, the pull toward new connections.

But chemistry doesn't answer the questions that determine whether a relationship lasts.

Who are you, actually, in a relationship? Not the version you present in the first months. The version that shows up when you're tired, stressed, or let down.

Who do you need to become for the relationship you say you want? This is the question where avoidance lives. Because becoming someone requires acknowledging a gap — and acknowledging a gap means sitting with something you haven't fully dealt with.

What do you actually want? Not what's reasonable. Not what makes sense given your age or circumstances. What you'd write down in first-draft mode, if no one was going to read it.

And what's honestly happening in your life right now that's shaping every decision you make about love?

Four questions. Most people answer the surface version of each one. The mirror pushes you to the real version.
