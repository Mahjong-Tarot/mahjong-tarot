Compatibility isn't just about signs. That's the part most people miss.

Even if you're in a compatible sign pairing — Tiger and Horse, Sheep and Rabbit, Dragon and Monkey — the five elements add another layer that most Western takes on Chinese astrology completely skip.

Two people can be compatible signs and still have significant elemental imbalance. One partner all fire, the other with none. One partner all Water — deep, introspective, slow-moving — paired with someone who's primarily Wood or Fire and needs movement and action. The sign says compatible. The element chart says: you're going to wear each other out.

In a Fire Horse year, element imbalance gets amplified the same way sign incompatibility does.

Think about what fire energy does at maximum intensity. People who have fire in their charts — Tiger, Horse, Snake, people born in fire-heavy years — are thriving in this energy. They feel alive this year. Everything is moving.

Their partners who have little or no fire in their charts are trying to keep up with an energy they don't naturally have access to. They're not wrong. They're not weak. They're just not built for maximum fire — and 2026 is running at maximum fire.

If you've noticed that one of you seems energized by this year while the other is exhausted or stressed, element imbalance might be a significant part of why.

This is the level of detail that a full chart reading gets into. Sign compatibility is the first pass. Element balance is the deeper picture.
